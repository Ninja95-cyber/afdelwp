ACADEMIC FORUM FOR DEVELOPMENT OF LESOTHO THINK TANK ONLINE CONFERENCE

A Pan-Africanist Outlook to the Life & Future of Lesotho as an Enclave of South Africa:

The People’s Voice in the Run-up to Lesotho 2022 National Election & Beyond

BACKGROUND

Lesotho and Republic of South Africa (RSA)’s geographical location places Lesotho in a position of an enclave, similar to that of only two other territories in the world; namely, the Republic of San Marino and the Vatican City, both of which are completely surrounded by their more powerful and richer neighbour, Italy. In order to ameliorate socio-economic implications that the status of these territories can have on the lives of peoples of Italy, Vatican and San Marino, there is an arrangement of open borders which allows free movement of people and commerce, with citizens of the three territories enjoying social and economic rights in all territories.

Between Lesotho and RSA, however, borders between the two countries are strictly monitored and controlled, including patrols by the army and other security personnel. It is Basotho who are at the receiving end of these containment measures as they cannot easily enter into the Republic to access socio-economic services of their better-endowed neighbour. It is a socio-economic relationship in which majority of Basotho suffer inequities that profoundly affect their ability to live quality lives in line with the set global Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). In terms of sustainability of the biophysical environment, water supply is a perhaps the most critical bonding factor between the two countries, yet wetlands and river sources from which this water comes, in Lesotho, are subjected to destructive anthropogenic pressures largely due to predominantly land depended economy in Lesotho.

EXPLAINING BASOTHO’S EXPERIENCES OF LESOTHO-RSA RELATIONS

There is little agreement on why Basotho’s experiences of their country’s relationship with RSA should be what they are. For some, the explanation is structural, and has to do with the quantity and quality of size of Basotho’s territory; namely, the limited territorial extent and inadequate economic endowments. For others, the country has lacked leadership that has failed in, at least, two ways. First, failure to marshal and distribute the country’s limited resources in an equitable manner. Second, lack of imaginativeness necessary to fully appreciate ways in which current Lesotho/RSA relations have a bearing on the Basotho’s ability to live better lives. In turn, this failure has meant that, over the last 50 years, Lesotho’s ruling elite have not explored ways in which current Lesotho/RSA relations can be changed with a view to relieve Basotho of hardships associated with the country’s internal socio-economic arrangements, on the one hand, and the current Lesotho/RSA relations, on the other.

However, all analysts and observers agree that some combination of Lesotho’s limited resources, on the one hand, and lack of imagination among those who have ruled the country, on the other, means that the state lacks capacity to:

  1. fund and manage public institutions in ways that can ensure good governance;
  2. provide society with adequate public services in health and education; and
  3. provide society with adequate socio-economic security.
Current relations between LS and RSA mean that Basotho cannot access these services and socio-economic security without enormous hardships at Lesotho/SA borders. As is known, one result of Lesotho’s structural and other circumstances is that, the country has experienced unceasing political instability since independence, and, in that time, majority of Basotho have suffered untold socio-economic insecurity that violate their fundamental right to human dignity.

With a view to explore an inclusive and sustainable development in Lesotho, the conference will:

(a) examine more in-depth explanations for Basotho’s experience of their country’s relationship with SA, and
(b) propose relationship, or relationships, that can best address inequities Basotho suffer under the current relationship between the two countries.

This Conference will provide a platform on which participants can examine the various aspects of the current relationship of Lesotho and RSA.

CONFERENCE THEME

Guided by the overarching theme, “A Pan-Africanist Outlook to the Life & Future of Lesotho as an Enclave of South Africa: The People’s Voice in the Run-up to Lesotho 2022 National Election & Beyond”, and in order to focus the Basotho electorate on pertinent sustainability issues for the 2022 national elections, the conference will focus on the following sub-themes:

  • A Pan-Africanist Outlook of the relationship between Lesotho & South Africa
  • Lesotho’s existence as an enclave of South Africa
  • Borders and Migration between Lesotho & South Africa
  • Political and Socio-Economic relationship between Lesotho & South Africa
  • Evolution of the current relationship between Lesotho & South Africa
  • The relationship between Lesotho & South Africa in relation quality Basic and Higher Education;
  • The relationship between Lesotho & South Africa in relation to quality health services;
  • The relationship between Lesotho & South Africa in relation to environmental sustainability, Tourism & Climate Change
  • The relationship between Lesotho & South Africa in relation to business & economy.
  • Any other sub-themes that speak to the overarching theme of this conference

INVITED PRESENTATIONS

We invite presentations in following formats.

  1. Long paper of up to 6000 words.
  2. Short paper of 1500 words.
  3. Snapshot paper of 1000 words.
  4. We welcome other forms of presentations that those wishing to participate may propose.
  5. Abstracts must be submitted by dd-mm-yyy. Please use the template provided to prepare for your abstracts and must be submitted through the EasyChair System.

PRESENTATION SESSIONS

All presentations session will be allowed forty minutes, of which 15 minutes shall be presentation of paper, and 25 minutes for questions/comments/discussion.

PRESENTATION FORMATS

1. Plenary Sessions

Conference Organisers will select papers that may be deemed to fit plenary sessions. This may be done by picking such papers from submissions of intending participants, or by inviting such papers from colleagues. Alternatively, Organisers may invite individuals who might be deemed qualified to speak in such a session, based on their specialty, or other consideration.

2. Parallel, Thematic Small Group Sessions

These will consist of presentation of a number of papers addressing a similar theme, or sub-theme, to a smaller audience.

CONFERENCE OUTPUTS

Resolutions and a Declaration on a viable political arrangement between Lesotho and RSA, with high prospects to improve the quality of life among the people of Lesotho and South Africa
